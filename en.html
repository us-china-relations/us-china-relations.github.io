About

About the Research Group

The US–China Relations Research Group launched in February 2026, hosted at the East Asia Institute (EAI). Chaired by Professor Chun Chaesung, the group brings together international-relations scholars from Seoul National University, Korea University, the Korea Institute for Defense Analyses (KIDA), and EAI.

Moving beyond a narrowly bilateral U.S.–China frame, the group examines how Korea, Europe, and other middle powers can jointly shape the norms and institutions of world order amid planetary challenges — climate change, nuclear proliferation, AI governance, pandemics. Readings span major IR theory (Modelski's long cycle; Schweller & Pu on the arts of resistance; Deudney & Ikenberry on democratic internationalism; Wiener, Lake & Risse on contestations of the liberal order) and current issues (nuclear strategy, minilateralism, comparative capability assessments).

The ultimate goal is to move beyond theoretical learning and to develop the practical vocabulary that Korea's diplomatic strategy and policy planning can draw on.
